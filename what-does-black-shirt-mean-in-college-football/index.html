What Does Black Shirt Mean in College Football?

During college football practices, coaches select players who demonstrate exceptional skills, leadership qualities, and hard work, and these players are awarded black shirts. The black shirt signifies that the player has earned the right to be part of the first-team squad.

The tradition of the black shirt began at the University of Nebraska in the 1960s. The team's defensive coach, Bob Devaney, began awarding black practice jerseys to players who showed exceptional skills in practice. The tradition continued, and the black shirt became a symbol of excellence.

Wearing a black shirt signifies that the player is an essential asset to the team and has earned their coach's respect and trust. It is a way for the coach to motivate their players to perform their best on the field and reward them for their hard work.
