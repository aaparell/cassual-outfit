The Thrilla in Manila

One of the most famous title fights in boxing history was the Thrilla in Manila, a heavyweight bout between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The two fighters had already squared off twice before, but this fight was different. The heat and humidity of the Philippines made for grueling conditions, and both fighters were pushed to their limits.

Despite the brutal nature of the fight, Ali emerged victorious after Frazier's trainer threw in the towel before the 15th round. The fight is still considered one of the greatest in boxing history, and it cemented Ali's status as one of the all-time greats.

The Rumble in the Jungle

Another iconic title fight was the Rumble in the Jungle, a heavyweight bout between Ali and George Foreman. The fight took place in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo) and was notable for Ali's "rope-a-dope" strategy, in which he allowed Foreman to tire himself out by throwing punches while Ali leaned against the ropes.

Despite being the underdog, Ali emerged victorious in the eighth round after Foreman was unable to continue. The fight is considered one of the greatest upsets in boxing history, and it solidified Ali's status as a legend of the sport.
