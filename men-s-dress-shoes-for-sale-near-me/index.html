Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between an oxford and a derby shoe?

A: The main difference between an oxford and a derby shoe is the way the laces are sewn. In an oxford shoe, the laces are sewn into the shoe's vamp, resulting in a sleeker, more formal look. In a derby shoe, the laces are sewn onto the shoe's quarters, resulting in a more casual look.

Q: How do I know if a shoe fits properly?

A: A properly fitting shoe should feel snug but not tight, with enough room in the toe box for your toes to wiggle. Your heel should also feel snug but not tight, with minimal slipping.

Q: What should I wear with my dress shoes?

A: Dress shoes can be worn with a variety of outfits, including suits, dress pants, chinos, and even jeans. When in doubt, opt for a classic look with a suit or dress pants and a button-down shirt.

Q: How can I tell if a shoe is high quality?

A: A high-quality shoe will have a few key features, including quality leather, sturdy construction, and attention to detail in the stitching and finishing. Look for shoes made from full-grain leather and with a Goodyear welt construction for added durability.
