The top hat, also known as a stovepipe hat, is a tall, cylindrical hat with a flat top and no brim. The hat was first introduced in the late 18th century and quickly became a symbol of wealth and power. The top hat was worn by royalty, politicians, and other influential figures, and was a staple of formal attire for many years.

The Evolution of the Top Hat

Over the years, the top hat has evolved in both style and function. In the early days, the hat was made of beaver fur and was quite heavy. How to Wear a Top Hat
