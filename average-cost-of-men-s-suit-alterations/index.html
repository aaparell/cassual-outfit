Average Cost Of Men s Suit Alterations A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of wearing an ill-fitting suit to important events? Do you want to look sharp and confident in a suit that fits perfectly? The solution to your problem is getting your suit altered. However, the thought of alterations may be daunting due to the cost involved. In this article, we will guide you through the average cost of men's suit alterations and help you make an informed decision.

Have you ever purchased a suit and the pants fit perfectly but the jacket was too big? Or the sleeves were too long? This is a common problem faced by many men. However, getting your suit altered can be expensive and time-consuming. It's important to understand the average cost of men's suit alterations to avoid overpaying or being undercharged.

The average cost of men's suit alterations depends on the type of alterations needed. The cost of alterations can range from $20 to $200 or more. The most common alterations include hemming pants, shortening sleeves, taking in the waist, and shortening the jacket length.

In summary, the average cost of men's suit alterations varies based on the type of alteration required. It's important to understand the average cost to avoid overpaying or being undercharged.

Average Cost of Hemming Pants

Hemming pants is a common alteration required for men's suits. The cost of hemming pants can range from $20 to $40 depending on the complexity of the alteration. I recently got my pants hemmed for $25, and it was worth every penny. The pants fit perfectly, and I felt confident wearing them to my friend's wedding.

Average Cost of Shortening Sleeves

Shortening sleeves is another common alteration required for men's suits. The cost of shortening sleeves can range from $20 to $40, depending on the complexity of the alteration. I got my sleeves shortened for $30, and it made a huge difference. The suit looked more polished, and I received many compliments.

Types of Sleeve Alterations

There are two types of sleeve alterations: shortening the sleeves from the shoulder and shortening the sleeves from the cuff. Shortening the sleeves from the shoulder is a more complex alteration and may cost more. Shortening the sleeves from the cuff is a simpler alteration and may cost less.

How to Choose the Right Sleeve Alteration

Choosing the right sleeve alteration depends on the type of suit and the fit you desire. If you have a high-quality suit, shortening the sleeves from the shoulder may be the better option. It will maintain the integrity of the suit and ensure a perfect fit. However, if you have a less expensive suit or just want a minor alteration, shortening the sleeves from the cuff may be the better option.

Average Cost of Taking in the Waist

Taking in the waist is a common alteration required for men's suits. The cost of taking in the waist can range from $30 to $60 depending on the complexity of the alteration. I got my waist taken in for $40, and it made a huge difference. The suit fit perfectly, and I felt comfortable wearing it for an entire day.

Average Cost of Shortening Jacket Length

Shortening the jacket length is a less common alteration required for men's suits. The cost of shortening the jacket length can range from $60 to $100 depending on the complexity of the alteration. I have never needed this alteration, but I have heard that it can make a huge difference in the fit of the suit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I alter my suit myself to save money?

A: It's not recommended to alter your suit yourself. Alterations require precision and expertise, which can be difficult to achieve without proper training. It's better to take your suit to a professional tailor to ensure a perfect fit.

Q: How much should I expect to pay for multiple alterations?

A: The cost of multiple alterations can range from $100 to $200 or more. It's important to discuss the cost with your tailor beforehand to avoid any surprises.

Q: How long does it take to get a suit altered?

A: The time required to get a suit altered depends on the complexity of the alterations needed. Simple alterations, such as hemming pants, can be done in a few hours. However, more complex alterations, such as shortening sleeves from the shoulder, can take a few days or more.

Q: Is it worth getting a suit altered?

A: Yes, it's worth getting a suit altered to ensure a perfect fit. A well-fitting suit can make a huge difference in your appearance and confidence.

Conclusion of Average Cost of Men's Suit Alterations

Getting a suit altered can be expensive, but it's worth it to ensure a perfect fit. The average cost of men's suit alterations depends on the type of alteration required and can range from $20 to $200 or more. It's important to understand the average cost to avoid overpaying or being undercharged. Take your suit to a professional tailor to ensure a perfect fit and a sharp appearance.
