Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between sustainable and ethical fashion?

A: Sustainable fashion refers to clothing that is made with eco-friendly materials and production processes, while ethical fashion refers to clothing that is made in a socially responsible way. Both sustainability and ethics are important considerations for creating a more responsible fashion industry.

Q: Are sustainable brands more expensive?

A: While sustainable brands may be more expensive upfront, they often have a longer lifespan and are made with higher quality materials. This means that in the long run, investing in sustainable clothing can save you money and reduce your environmental impact.

Q: Can fast fashion brands be sustainable?

A: While it is possible for fast fashion brands to adopt more sustainable practices, the fast fashion business model itself is inherently unsustainable. Fast fashion prioritizes quantity over quality, and relies on low-cost labor and unsustainable materials to produce clothing at a rapid pace. Instead of supporting fast fashion brands, consider investing in sustainable alternatives that prioritize environmental and social responsibility.

Q: How can I find sustainable brands on Zalando?

A: Zalando has a dedicated sustainable fashion section on their website, where you can browse sustainable brands and learn more about their sustainable practices. You can also look for sustainable certifications like GOTS or Fairtrade to ensure that the brands you are choosing prioritize sustainability and ethical practices.
