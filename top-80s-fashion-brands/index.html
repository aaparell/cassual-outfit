Top 80S Fashion Brands

Written by Aprilia Jul 23, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you want to rock the retro look with the top 80s fashion brands? The fashion scene in the 80s was bold, colorful, and loud. From acid-washed denim to neon colors, the 80s fashion was all about making a statement. In this blog post, we will explore the top 80s fashion brands and how they continue to influence the fashion industry today.

The 80s fashion scene was not without its flaws. The bold and loud fashion choices often came at the expense of comfort and functionality. However, the unique and daring fashion choices also paved the way for experimentation and self-expression.

If you are looking to incorporate 80s style into your wardrobe, you need to know the top 80s fashion brands that defined the era.

In this blog post, we will explore the top 80s fashion brands, their target audience, and how they continue to influence the fashion industry today.

1. Calvin Klein

Calvin Klein was one of the most popular fashion brands in the 80s. The brand was known for its minimalistic and sleek designs that were loved by both men and women. The target audience for Calvin Klein was the urban youth who wanted to make a fashion statement without going over the top.

Personally, I remember my first pair of Calvin Klein jeans that I wore to my high school prom. They were simple yet stylish and made me feel confident and fashionable. Today, Calvin Klein continues to be a popular brand and has expanded its product line to include fragrances, accessories, and even home decor.

2. Guess

Guess was another popular 80s fashion brand that is still going strong today. The brand was known for its denim clothing, which included acid-washed jeans and denim jackets. The target audience for Guess was the young and trendy who wanted to make a bold fashion statement.

Personally, I remember wearing my first pair of Guess jeans to a school dance. They were tight-fitting and had a high waist, which was the style back then. Today, Guess still offers a variety of denim clothing and has expanded its product line to include accessories and fragrances.

The Influence of 80s Fashion Brands Today

The 80s fashion brands have had a significant impact on the fashion industry today. The bold and daring fashion choices of the 80s have paved the way for experimentation and self-expression. Today, we see designers incorporating 80s-inspired designs into their collections, from neon colors to oversized silhouettes.

How to Incorporate 80s Fashion into Your Wardrobe Today

If you want to incorporate 80s fashion into your wardrobe today, there are several ways to do so. Start with simple pieces, such as a pair of acid-washed jeans or a denim jacket. You can also add neon accessories, such as a belt or earrings, to add a pop of color to your outfit.

3. Versace

Versace was a high-end fashion brand that was popular in the 80s. The brand was known for its bold and colorful designs that were loved by celebrities and fashion icons. The target audience for Versace was the elite and wealthy who could afford the high-end fashion.

Personally, I remember seeing Versace designs on the red carpet and being in awe of the intricate designs and bold colors. Today, Versace continues to be a high-end fashion brand and has expanded its product line to include fragrances, accessories, and home decor.

4. Nike

Nike was a popular sportswear brand in the 80s. The brand was known for its comfortable and functional athletic wear that was loved by both athletes and fashion enthusiasts. The target audience for Nike was the young and active who wanted to look stylish while staying comfortable.

Personally, I remember wearing my first pair of Nike sneakers to a school basketball game. They were comfortable and stylish and made me feel confident on the court. Today, Nike continues to be a popular sportswear brand and has expanded its product line to include casual wear and accessories.

Conclusion of Top 80s Fashion Brands

The 80s fashion brands continue to influence the fashion industry today. From Calvin Klein to Nike, these brands have made a significant impact on the fashion world with their unique and daring designs. If you want to incorporate 80s fashion into your wardrobe today, start with simple pieces and add a pop of color or pattern to make a statement.
