1. Black Suit

As the saying goes, "Every man should own a black suit." This is because black is a timeless color that symbolizes elegance, sophistication, and power. It is the go-to color for formal events like weddings, funerals, and black-tie events.

Personally, I remember wearing my first black suit to my sister's wedding. I felt confident and stylish, and it made me stand out in a sea of navy and gray suits. However, it is important to note that black suits are not suitable for daytime events or less formal occasions.

How to Choose the Right Suit Color?

When choosing a suit color, there are a few things to consider, such as your skin tone, the occasion, and your personal style. Here are some tips to help you choose the right suit color:

  • For fair skin tones, choose lighter colors like beige, light gray, and light blue.
  • For medium skin tones, choose colors like navy, charcoal gray, and burgundy.
  • For dark skin tones, choose colors like black, dark gray, and royal blue.
  • For formal events, choose classic colors like black, navy, and charcoal gray.
  • For casual events, choose lighter colors like beige, light gray, and olive green.
  • For statement-making events, choose bold colors like white, burgundy, and royal blue.

How to Style Your Suit?

Once you have chosen your suit color, it's time to style it. Here are some tips to help you style your suit:

  • Pair your suit with a crisp white shirt for a classic look.
  • Add a pop of color with a patterned tie or pocket square.
  • Choose the right shoes to complement your suit color. Black shoes go well with black and navy suits, while brown shoes go well with brown and beige suits.
  • Make sure your suit fits well. A well-fitted suit will make you look confident and stylish.
