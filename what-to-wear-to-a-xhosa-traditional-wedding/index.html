What To Wear To A Xhosa Traditional Wedding A Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Attending a Xhosa traditional wedding can be an exciting experience, but figuring out what to wear can be a bit daunting. You want to ensure that you are dressed appropriately and respectfully for the occasion, while also showing off your personal style. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about what to wear to a Xhosa traditional wedding.

One of the main concerns when attending a Xhosa traditional wedding is cultural appropriation. It's important to respect the culture and traditions of the Xhosa people and avoid appropriating their clothing or accessories. Additionally, you want to dress appropriately for the occasion and adhere to any dress codes specified by the couple or their families.

When attending a Xhosa traditional wedding, it's customary to wear traditional African attire. For men, this typically means a shirt, pants, and a jacket made from a traditional African fabric such as shweshwe or wax print. Women often wear a dress or skirt and blouse made from the same fabrics. It's common to accessorize with beaded jewelry and headwear, such as a gele or doek.

In summary, when attending a Xhosa traditional wedding, it's important to dress appropriately and respectfully. This typically means wearing traditional African attire made from fabrics such as shweshwe or wax print. Accessorizing with beaded jewelry and headwear is also common.

The Importance of Traditional Attire

As a Xhosa person, traditional attire is an important part of my culture and identity. When attending a Xhosa traditional wedding, it's important to wear traditional attire as a sign of respect and appreciation for the culture. Not only does it show respect for the couple and their families, but it also allows you to fully immerse yourself in the experience and celebrate the traditions of the Xhosa people.

Choosing the Right Attire

When choosing what to wear to a Xhosa traditional wedding, it's important to consider the dress code specified by the couple or their families. If a specific dress code is not specified, it's safe to assume that traditional African attire is appropriate. It's also important to consider the weather and venue when choosing your attire. For example, if the wedding is being held outdoors in the summer, you may want to opt for a lighter fabric and more breathable attire.

Women's Attire

For women, traditional African attire for a Xhosa traditional wedding typically consists of a dress or skirt and blouse made from shweshwe or wax print fabric. The dress or skirt can be long or short, depending on personal preference and the dress code specified. It's also common to accessorize with beaded jewelry and headwear such as a gele or doek. When choosing your attire, it's important to consider the colors and patterns of the fabric. Bright colors and bold patterns are common in traditional African attire.

Men's Attire

For men, traditional attire for a Xhosa traditional wedding typically consists of a shirt, pants, and jacket made from shweshwe or wax print fabric. The shirt can be short or long sleeve, and the pants can be tailored or looser-fitting. The jacket can be a blazer or a more traditional African-style jacket. It's also common to accessorize with beaded jewelry and headwear such as a fedora or beanie.

My Personal Experience

As a Xhosa person, attending traditional weddings is a common occurrence in my family. When attending a Xhosa traditional wedding, I typically wear a dress made from shweshwe or wax print fabric. I accessorize with beaded jewelry and a gele headwrap. For men in my family, they typically wear a shirt, pants, and jacket made from the same fabric. We always ensure that we are dressed respectfully and appropriately for the occasion.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a suit to a Xhosa traditional wedding?

A: It's not recommended to wear a suit to a Xhosa traditional wedding, as traditional African attire is more appropriate and respectful.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a Xhosa traditional wedding?

A: It's best to avoid wearing jeans to a Xhosa traditional wedding, as they are not considered appropriate attire for the occasion.

Q: Do I have to wear a gele or doek?

A: While it's not required to wear a gele or doek, it is a common accessory worn by women attending a Xhosa traditional wedding.

Q: Can I wear traditional African attire from a different culture?

A: It's best to wear traditional African attire that is specific to the Xhosa culture, as wearing attire from a different culture can be seen as cultural appropriation.

Conclusion

When attending a Xhosa traditional wedding, it's important to dress appropriately and respectfully. This typically means wearing traditional African attire made from fabrics such as shweshwe or wax print. Accessorizing with beaded jewelry and headwear is also common. By following these guidelines, you can ensure that you are showing respect for the culture and traditions of the Xhosa people while also celebrating the joyous occasion with the couple and their families.
