Authentic Italian Lasagna Recipe Bechamel A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 11, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for an authentic Italian lasagna recipe that will wow your friends and family? Do you want to impress them with a homemade bechamel sauce that takes your lasagna to the next level? Look no further! This tutorial will guide you through the process of making a delicious lasagna with a creamy bechamel sauce that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is loved all over the world. However, making an authentic lasagna can be challenging, especially when it comes to the bechamel sauce. Many people struggle with getting the sauce just right, resulting in a dry and unappetizing lasagna. This is where our tutorial comes in. We will provide you with all the tips and tricks you need to make a perfect bechamel sauce for your lasagna.

The target of this tutorial is to provide you with a step-by-step guide on making an authentic Italian lasagna with a bechamel sauce. We will cover everything from the ingredients you need to the preparation process and the cooking instructions. We will also provide you with some helpful tips to ensure that your lasagna turns out perfectly every time.

In summary, this tutorial will guide you through the process of making an authentic Italian lasagna with a bechamel sauce. We will provide you with all the information you need to make a delicious and impressive lasagna that your friends and family will love.

What is Bechamel Sauce?

I first discovered bechamel sauce when I was living in Italy. It is a creamy white sauce that is made with butter, flour, milk, and nutmeg. Bechamel is a staple in Italian cuisine and is used in many dishes, including lasagna. The sauce is easy to make, but it requires some attention to detail to ensure that it turns out perfectly.

The Ingredients You Will Need

To make an authentic Italian lasagna with bechamel sauce, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 lb lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Noodles

The first step in making lasagna is to cook the noodles. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add the lasagna noodles and cook them according to the package instructions. Drain the noodles and set them aside.

Tip:

To prevent the noodles from sticking together, add a tablespoon of olive oil to the water while boiling.

Step 2: Prepare the Meat Sauce

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until the onion is translucent. Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, water, salt, pepper, basil, and oregano. Simmer the mixture for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Tip:

For a vegetarian option, you can substitute the ground beef with diced vegetables, such as zucchini, mushrooms, or eggplant.

Step 3: Make the Bechamel Sauce

In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk until smooth. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Add the nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Continue to whisk until the sauce thickens, about 10-15 minutes.

Tip:

Be patient when making the bechamel sauce. Whisking constantly and gradually adding the milk will prevent lumps from forming.

Step 4: Assemble the Lasagna

Preheat the oven to 375°F. In a large baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom. Add a layer of lasagna noodles. Spread a layer of bechamel sauce over the noodles. Repeat the layers of meat sauce, noodles, and bechamel sauce until all the ingredients are used up. Top the lasagna with the grated Parmesan cheese.

Tip:

To make the lasagna easier to cut and serve, let it cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing.

Step 5: Bake the Lasagna

Cover the lasagna with foil and bake it for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Tip:

If the cheese is not golden enough, broil the lasagna for the last 2-3 minutes.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I make lasagna in advance?

A: Yes, you can assemble the lasagna a day ahead and refrigerate it until ready to bake. You may need to add an extra 10-15 minutes of baking time.

Q: Can I freeze lasagna?

A: Yes, you can freeze lasagna. Assemble the lasagna as usual, but do not bake it. Cover it tightly with foil and freeze for up to 3 months. When ready to bake, thaw the lasagna in the refrigerator for 24 hours, then bake it according to the recipe instructions.

Q: Can I use a different type of meat?

A: Yes, you can use ground turkey, chicken, or pork instead of ground beef.

Q: Can I use a different type of cheese?

A: Yes, you can use mozzarella, ricotta, or a combination of different cheeses instead of Parmesan.

Conclusion of Authentic Italian Lasagna Recipe Bechamel

Congratulations! You have successfully made an authentic Italian lasagna with a delicious bechamel sauce. With these step-by-step instructions and helpful tips, you can now impress your friends and family with your homemade lasagna. Buon appetito!
