Famous Food Places In Indianapolis A Guide To The Best Eats

Written by Bardi Jul 14, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a foodie looking for the best places to eat in Indianapolis? Do you want to explore the local food scene and taste some of the city's most iconic dishes? Look no further! In this guide, we will take you through some of the most famous food places in Indianapolis, where you can indulge in delicious meals and satisfy your cravings.

Indianapolis has a vibrant food culture, with a wide range of culinary options to choose from. However, with so many restaurants and eateries to pick from, it can be overwhelming to decide where to go. That's where this guide comes in – we will help you navigate the city's food scene and highlight the must-visit places that you cannot afford to miss.

Whether you're a local or a visitor, this guide to famous food places in Indianapolis will help you discover new and exciting dishes, satisfy your taste buds, and enjoy a memorable culinary experience.

St. Elmo Steak House

St. Elmo Steak House is a legendary restaurant in Indianapolis, known for its classic steakhouse fare, signature shrimp cocktail, and old-world charm. The restaurant has been serving patrons since 1902 and has become an iconic destination for foodies in Indianapolis.

I had the pleasure of dining at St. Elmo Steak House last year, and it was an unforgettable experience. The shrimp cocktail was the highlight of my meal – it was spicy, tangy, and full of flavor. I also enjoyed the steak, which was cooked to perfection and served with a side of mashed potatoes and green beans.

If you're a fan of steak or seafood, St. Elmo Steak House is a must-visit restaurant in Indianapolis. Be sure to make a reservation in advance, as it can get crowded.

Milktooth

Milktooth is a trendy brunch spot in Indianapolis, known for its creative menu, farm-to-table ingredients, and hipster vibes. The restaurant has won numerous awards and has been featured in several publications, including Bon Appétit, Eater, and The New York Times.

I visited Milktooth on a Sunday morning, and it was packed with people waiting to be seated. However, the wait was worth it – the food was delicious, and the atmosphere was lively and fun. I tried the Dutch Baby pancake, which was fluffy, buttery, and topped with fresh berries and whipped cream.

If you're looking for a unique brunch experience in Indianapolis, Milktooth is the place to be. The menu changes frequently, so be sure to check their website for the latest offerings.

Shapiro's Delicatessen

Shapiro's Delicatessen is a family-owned restaurant in Indianapolis, known for its Jewish-style deli sandwiches, matzo ball soup, and homemade desserts. The restaurant has been serving customers since 1905 and has become a beloved institution in the city.

I visited Shapiro's Delicatessen for lunch and tried their famous corned beef Reuben sandwich. The sandwich was piled high with tender corned beef, sauerkraut, Swiss cheese, and Thousand Island dressing, all served on rye bread. It was one of the best sandwiches I've ever had.

If you're in the mood for a classic deli sandwich or some comforting soup, Shapiro's Delicatessen is the place to go. The portions are generous, and the prices are reasonable.

Long's Bakery

Long's Bakery is a local institution in Indianapolis, known for its fresh, homemade doughnuts and pastries. The bakery has been in business since 1955 and has become a favorite spot for sweet treats in the city.

I visited Long's Bakery on a Saturday morning and was blown away by the selection of doughnuts. I tried the glazed yeast doughnut, which was light, fluffy, and melt-in-your-mouth delicious. I also tried the apple fritter, which was crispy on the outside and filled with chunks of fresh apple.

If you have a sweet tooth and love doughnuts, Long's Bakery is a must-visit spot in Indianapolis. Be sure to get there early, as the doughnuts sell out quickly.

Conclusion of Famous Food Places in Indianapolis

Indianapolis is a food lover's paradise, with a diverse range of culinary options to choose from. Whether you're in the mood for a classic steakhouse meal, a trendy brunch spot, a comforting deli sandwich, or some delicious doughnuts, the city has something to offer.

By visiting these famous food places in Indianapolis, you can experience the city's food culture and indulge in some of its most iconic dishes. So, what are you waiting for? Grab your appetite and head to one of these must-visit spots today!

A: The most famous dish at St. Elmo Steak House is their signature shrimp cocktail, which is known for its spicy and flavorful sauce.
