What Do They Wear In India A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

India is a country with a rich culture and diverse traditions. One of the most fascinating aspects of Indian culture is the traditional clothing worn by the people. If you're planning a trip to India or just curious about the clothing worn by Indians, then this guide is for you.

Are you unsure about what to pack for your trip to India? Do you want to make sure you're dressing appropriately for the culture? Do you want to avoid standing out as a tourist? If so, then read on to learn more about what to wear in India.

What Do They Wear in India?

Traditional Indian clothing varies by region and religion, but some common items of clothing worn by both men and women include:

  • Saris: A long piece of fabric draped around the body, worn with a blouse and petticoat
  • Salwar Kameez: A loose tunic top worn over loose pants
  • Dhoti Kurta: A long shirt worn over a cloth wrapped around the legs
  • Kurti: A tunic top worn with leggings or pants
  • Turbans: Worn by men, usually made of cloth and wrapped around the head

In addition to traditional clothing, western-style clothing is also popular in India, especially among younger generations. However, it's important to note that modesty is highly valued in Indian culture, so clothing that covers the shoulders and knees is recommended.

My Personal Experience with Indian Clothing

During my trip to India, I was fascinated by the beautiful saris worn by women. I decided to purchase one for myself and was surprised at how comfortable it was to wear. I also found that wearing traditional Indian clothing helped me blend in more with the locals and feel more immersed in the culture.

Tips for Dressing Appropriately in India

When visiting India, it's important to dress appropriately for the culture. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose clothing that covers the shoulders and knees
  • Avoid tight or revealing clothing
  • Wear comfortable, breathable fabrics
  • Carry a scarf or shawl to cover your head or shoulders if needed
  • Remove shoes before entering temples or homes

Understanding Regional Clothing

As mentioned earlier, traditional clothing varies by region in India. For example, in Rajasthan, men wear a type of turban called a pagri, while women wear colorful lehengas. In Kerala, the traditional clothing for women is a white saree with a gold border, while men wear a white mundu and shirt.

How to Tie a Sari

If you're interested in wearing a sari during your trip to India, here are the basic steps:

  1. Start by tucking the end of the sari into your petticoat at the waist
  2. Wrap the sari around your waist, making sure to leave enough fabric to create pleats in the front
  3. Create pleats in the front and tuck them into your petticoat
  4. Wrap the remaining fabric around your waist and over your shoulder, creating a drape over your left arm
  5. Secure the drape with safety pins

FAQs About What Do They Wear in India

Q: Is it appropriate to wear shorts in India?

A: It's best to avoid wearing shorts in India, as they are considered too revealing. Opt for loose pants or long skirts instead.

Q: Can I wear western-style clothing in India?

A: Yes, western-style clothing is accepted in India, but it's still important to dress modestly.

Q: Do I need to wear a head covering in India?

A: It depends on the region and the occasion. In some parts of India, it's customary for women to cover their heads, while in other regions it's not necessary.

Q: What should I wear when visiting a temple?

A: When visiting a temple, it's important to dress conservatively. Remove your shoes before entering and cover your head and shoulders if necessary.

Conclusion of What Do They Wear in India

India is a country with a rich and diverse culture, and traditional clothing is an important part of that culture. By dressing appropriately for the culture and understanding regional variations, you can show respect for the locals and immerse yourself in the culture. Whether you choose to wear traditional Indian clothing or opt for western-style clothing, remember to dress modestly and comfortably.
