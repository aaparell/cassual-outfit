How To Wear Saree In Traditional Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you attending a traditional Indian wedding or event and wondering how to wear a saree in a traditional style? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many women struggle with the process of draping a saree in a traditional style, but with a little guidance, it can be easy and enjoyable.

Wearing a saree in a traditional style can be a daunting task, especially for those who are not used to wearing this type of clothing. However, with a few simple steps, you can easily master the art of draping a saree in a traditional style.

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear a saree in a traditional style:

Step 1: Choose your saree

Choosing the right saree is the first and most important step in the process. Make sure to pick a saree that suits your body type and complements your skin tone. Also, make sure the saree is long enough to wrap around your body comfortably.

Step 2: Wear a blouse

Wear a blouse that fits well and complements the saree. Make sure the blouse is not too tight or too loose, as this can affect the way the saree looks when draped. The blouse should be worn with the opening at the back.

Step 3: Tie the petticoat

A petticoat is worn under the saree to give it shape and support. Tie the petticoat tightly around your waist and make sure it sits comfortably on your hips.

Step 4: Start draping the saree

Hold the saree at one end and tuck it into the petticoat at the center of your waist. Make sure the saree is tucked in tightly and neatly. Start wrapping the saree around your waist, making sure the pleats are even and neat.

Step 5: Make the pleats

Make 5-6 even pleats at the front of the saree and tuck them into the petticoat. Make sure the pleats are neat and even, and the borders match up.

Step 6: Drape the pallu

Take the remaining portion of the saree and drape it over your left shoulder, making sure the border is facing down. Pin the pallu to your blouse at the left shoulder, making sure it falls neatly.

Step 7: Complete the look

Add some finishing touches to complete the look. Wear some jewelry, such as earrings or a necklace, and apply some makeup if you wish.

Conclusion of How to Wear Saree in Traditional Style

Wearing a saree in a traditional style can be an intimidating process at first, but with a little practice, anyone can master the art of draping a saree. Remember to choose the right saree, wear a well-fitted blouse, tie the petticoat tightly, and make neat pleats. With these simple steps, you'll be able to wear a saree in a traditional style with confidence and grace.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a saree without a petticoat?

A: No, a petticoat is necessary to give the saree shape and support.

Q: What type of jewelry should I wear with a saree?

A: You can wear a variety of jewelry with a saree, including earrings, necklaces, bangles, and bracelets. Choose jewelry that complements the saree and your personal style.

Q: How do I know if the saree is too long?

A: If the saree is too long, it will be difficult to wrap around your waist and make neat pleats. Make sure the saree is the right length for your height and body type.

Q: How do I keep the saree in place?

A: You can use safety pins to keep the saree in place, especially around the pleats and pallu. Make sure to use small, discreet pins that won't be visible.
