Indian Wedding Dress Types A Guide To Finding The Perfect Outfit

Written by Frank Oct 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, intricate designs, and beautiful fabrics. The wedding dress is one of the most important parts of any Indian wedding, and choosing the right one can be a daunting task. With so many different types of Indian wedding dresses to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this guide, we'll break down the different types of Indian wedding dresses and help you find the perfect outfit for your big day.

When it comes to Indian wedding dress types, there are a few common pain points that many brides-to-be face. Some may feel overwhelmed by the sheer number of options available, while others may struggle to find a dress that fits their personal style and budget. Additionally, cultural and religious considerations may come into play when choosing an Indian wedding dress.

The target of this guide is to provide a comprehensive overview of the different types of Indian wedding dresses available, as well as tips for choosing the right outfit for your needs. We'll cover everything from traditional sarees and lehengas to modern fusion wear, and provide insights into the cultural and religious significance of each type of dress.

In summary, this guide will provide a detailed look at the different types of Indian wedding dresses available, along with tips for choosing the right outfit for your needs. From traditional to modern, we'll cover it all!

Sarees

As one of the most traditional types of Indian wedding dresses, sarees are a popular choice for brides who want to embrace their culture and heritage on their big day. Sarees are long, flowing garments that are draped around the body and over the shoulder, creating a stunning and elegant look.

Personally, I wore a beautiful red and gold saree for my own wedding. It was made of silk and had intricate embroidery and beading that shimmered in the light. I felt like a true Indian princess!

When choosing a saree for your wedding, it's important to consider the fabric, color, and style. Silk is a popular choice for Indian wedding sarees, as it is luxurious and elegant. Colors like red, gold, and green are often associated with Indian weddings, but you can also choose a color that reflects your personal style and preferences.

Lehengas

Another popular type of Indian wedding dress is the lehenga. This is a three-piece ensemble that typically includes a long skirt, a blouse, and a dupatta (a long scarf or shawl). Lehengas come in a variety of styles and designs, from traditional to modern.

For my sister's wedding, I wore a stunning pink and gold lehenga that was covered in intricate embroidery and beading. It was a modern take on a traditional design, and I felt both comfortable and stylish in it.

When choosing a lehenga for your wedding, consider the fabric, color, and style. Traditional lehengas are often made of silk or other luxurious fabrics, while modern designs may incorporate more lightweight materials like chiffon or georgette. It's also important to consider the embroidery and beading, as this can add a lot of visual interest to the outfit.

Anarkalis

Anarkalis are a type of dress that originated in the Mughal era and have since become a popular choice for Indian weddings. They are typically long, flowing dresses that feature a fitted bodice and a flared skirt. They can be made of a variety of fabrics, from silk to cotton to chiffon.

For a friend's wedding, I wore a beautiful navy blue and gold Anarkali that had intricate embroidery and beading. It was both comfortable and stylish, and I received many compliments on it throughout the night.

When choosing an Anarkali for your wedding, consider the fabric, color, and style. Traditional Anarkalis are often made of silk and feature intricate embroidery and beading. However, modern designs may incorporate more lightweight materials and simpler designs.

Fusion Wear

For brides who want to embrace both their Indian heritage and their modern sensibilities, fusion wear can be a great option. Fusion wear combines traditional Indian elements with modern design elements to create a unique and stylish look.

For my cousin's wedding, I wore a stunning fusion wear outfit that included a long skirt, a blouse, and a jacket. The skirt was made of a traditional Indian fabric, while the blouse and jacket had more modern designs and cuts. It was a perfect blend of traditional and modern, and I felt both comfortable and stylish in it.

When choosing fusion wear for your wedding, consider the balance between traditional and modern elements. You want to create a look that feels unique and stylish, but also reflects your cultural heritage.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a saree or lehenga if I'm not Indian?

A: Absolutely! Indian wedding dresses are not exclusive to Indian brides. Many non-Indian brides choose to wear sarees or lehengas for their weddings as a way to embrace Indian culture and add some visual interest to their outfit.

Q: How much should I expect to spend on an Indian wedding dress?

A: The cost of an Indian wedding dress can vary widely depending on the fabric, design, and level of customization. Traditional silk sarees and lehengas can range from $500 to $5,000 or more, while modern fusion wear may be less expensive. It's important to set a budget before you start shopping and to stick to it.

Q: Do I need to consider cultural or religious factors when choosing an Indian wedding dress?

A: Yes, it's important to consider your cultural and religious background when choosing an Indian wedding dress. For example, certain colors may be more auspicious in some cultures, while certain styles may be more appropriate for religious ceremonies. It's always a good idea to consult with family members or religious leaders to ensure that your outfit is appropriate for your wedding.

Q: Can I customize my Indian wedding dress?

A: Yes, many designers and boutiques offer customization options for Indian wedding dresses. This can include choosing the fabric, color, embroidery, and beading. If you have a specific vision in mind for your wedding dress, it's worth exploring customization options to ensure that you get exactly what you want.

Conclusion of Indian Wedding Dress Types

Choosing the perfect Indian wedding dress can be a daunting task, but with this guide, you should have a better understanding of the different types of dresses available and what to consider when choosing one. Whether you opt for a traditional saree or lehenga, a modern fusion wear outfit, or a unique Anarkali, your wedding dress should reflect your personal style and cultural heritage. With a little bit of research and planning, you can find the perfect Indian wedding dress for your big day.
