Top 10 Fashion Designers Of All Time

Written by Aprilia Oct 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Do you ever wonder who are the top fashion designers of all time? Fashion designers play a significant role in the fashion industry, creating unique and innovative designs that set the trends for each season. From haute couture to ready-to-wear, these designers have made a lasting impact on the fashion industry and continue to inspire new generations of designers.

When it comes to fashion, everyone has their unique style and preferences. However, there are a few designers who have stood the test of time and continue to be recognized for their contributions to the fashion world.

In this article, we will discuss the top 10 fashion designers of all time, their contributions to the fashion industry, and some interesting facts about their lives and careers.

1. Coco Chanel

Coco Chanel is one of the most iconic fashion designers of all time, known for her elegant and timeless designs that revolutionized women's fashion in the 1920s. She introduced the little black dress, the Chanel suit, and the quilted handbag, which became must-have items for every fashion-conscious woman. Chanel's signature style was simple, sophisticated, and comfortable, with a focus on clean lines and high-quality materials.

Fun fact: Chanel was born in a poorhouse and started her career as a seamstress before becoming a fashion designer.

2. Yves Saint Laurent

Yves Saint Laurent was a visionary fashion designer who transformed the way women dressed in the 1960s and 1970s. He introduced the first tuxedo suit for women, the safari jacket, and the sheer blouse, among other groundbreaking designs. Saint Laurent's designs were known for their androgynous and daring style, pushing the boundaries of gender norms and empowering women to dress in a more masculine way.

Fun fact: Saint Laurent was the youngest person to be appointed as the head designer at the House of Dior in 1957, at the age of 21.

3. Christian Dior

Christian Dior was a French fashion designer who founded the House of Dior in 1946, and his first collection became an instant success, earning him the nickname "The New Look." Dior's designs were characterized by their feminine silhouettes, luxurious fabrics, and intricate details, which captured the glamour and romance of post-war Paris.

Fun fact: Dior's famous Bar Suit, which was part of his first collection, required 20 yards of fabric and had a waistline of only 19 inches.

4. Gianni Versace

Gianni Versace was an Italian fashion designer who founded the iconic fashion house Versace in 1978. Versace's designs were known for their bold prints, vibrant colors, and sexy cuts, which embodied the spirit of the 1980s and 1990s. He dressed some of the biggest celebrities of his time, including Princess Diana, Madonna, and Elton John.

Fun fact: Versace's signature Medusa logo was inspired by the floor of the ruins of an ancient Roman villa.

5. Ralph Lauren

Ralph Lauren is an American fashion designer who founded the fashion brand Polo Ralph Lauren in 1967. Lauren's designs were inspired by classic American style, featuring preppy and sporty elements that became synonymous with the brand. He is also known for his philanthropic work, including the creation of the Ralph Lauren Center for Cancer Care and Prevention in Harlem.

Fun fact: Lauren's real name is Ralph Lifshitz, but he changed it to Lauren when he was a teenager to avoid being teased for his last name.

6. Alexander McQueen

Alexander McQueen was a British fashion designer known for his avant-garde and theatrical designs. McQueen's designs were characterized by their bold prints, unconventional materials, and dramatic silhouettes, which challenged traditional notions of beauty and fashion. He was also known for his elaborate runway shows, which often included controversial and provocative themes.

Fun fact: McQueen's first job in the fashion industry was as an apprentice at Savile Row, where he learned the craft of tailoring.

7. Jean Paul Gaultier

Jean Paul Gaultier is a French fashion designer known for his daring and unconventional designs. Gaultier's designs were characterized by their playful and irreverent style, which challenged traditional notions of gender and beauty. He is also known for his collaborations with Madonna, designing some of her most iconic stage costumes.

Fun fact: Gaultier's first job in the fashion industry was as an assistant to Pierre Cardin.

8. Marc Jacobs

Marc Jacobs is an American fashion designer who founded his eponymous brand in 1984. Jacobs's designs were known for their eclectic and whimsical style, incorporating elements of pop culture and streetwear. He is also known for his collaborations with artists and musicians, including Takashi Murakami and Lady Gaga.

Fun fact: Jacobs's first job in the fashion industry was as a stock boy at a clothing boutique in New York City.

9. Valentino Garavani

Valentino Garavani is an Italian fashion designer who founded the fashion house Valentino in 1959. Valentino's designs were known for their romantic and feminine style, featuring delicate fabrics, intricate embroideries, and bold colors. He dressed some of the biggest stars of his time, including Elizabeth Taylor, Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis, and Audrey Hepburn.

Fun fact: Valentino's signature color is Valentino Red, which he described as "a color that makes everyone look good."

10. Hubert de Givenchy

Hubert de Givenchy was a French fashion designer who founded the fashion house Givenchy in 1952. Givenchy's designs were known for their elegant and refined style, featuring clean lines, luxurious fabrics, and intricate details. He dressed some of the most iconic women of his time, including Audrey Hepburn, Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis, and Grace Kelly.

Fun fact: Givenchy's famous "little black dress" for Audrey Hepburn in the movie "Breakfast at Tiffany's" sold for $923,187 at an auction in 2006.

Conclusion of Top 10 Fashion Designers of All Time

These top 10 fashion designers of all time have left a lasting impact on the fashion industry, inspiring new generations of designers and shaping the way we dress today. Their designs have become iconic and timeless, reflecting the changing trends and styles of their respective eras. Whether you prefer classic elegance or bold and daring style, there is a fashion designer on this list for everyone.
