Discovering Old Sunglasses Brands A Blast From The Past

Written by Daniel Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old sunglasses brands that everyone seems to be wearing these days? Do you long for a unique style that sets you apart from the crowd? If so, it's time to consider old sunglasses brands. These vintage shades are a blast from the past, offering a look that is both stylish and timeless. Not to mention, they often come with a fascinating history that makes them even more captivating.

When it comes to old sunglasses brands, there are many different targets. Some people are interested in the fashion aspect, while others are drawn to the historical significance. Regardless of your reason for exploring old sunglasses brands, there is sure to be something out there that catches your eye.

In this article, we will explore some of the most popular old sunglasses brands and what makes them so special. We will also provide tips for finding and caring for these vintage treasures. So, without further ado, let's dive in!

Ray-Ban: The Iconic Aviator

When it comes to old sunglasses brands, Ray-Ban is perhaps the most well-known. Founded in 1936, the company originally produced sunglasses for pilots in the US Army Air Corps. However, it wasn't until the 1950s that Ray-Ban's iconic Aviator style became popular with the general public. Today, the Aviator remains a classic design that is instantly recognizable.

Personally, I love my vintage Ray-Ban Aviators that I found at a thrift store. They have a timeless look that never goes out of style and they are incredibly durable. Plus, I feel like a total badass when I wear them.

Maui Jim: The Hawaiian Connection

While not as old as some of the other brands on this list, Maui Jim has been producing sunglasses for over 30 years. What sets this brand apart is its connection to Hawaii, where the company is based. Maui Jim's sunglasses are designed to protect your eyes from the harsh Hawaiian sun, making them perfect for outdoor activities like surfing and hiking.

I purchased a pair of vintage Maui Jim sunglasses at a flea market and was pleasantly surprised by their quality. They are incredibly lightweight and comfortable to wear, yet still provide excellent protection from the sun.

Revo: The Revolutionary Lens

Founded in 1985, Revo is a relatively new player in the world of old sunglasses brands. However, the company made a name for itself with its revolutionary lens technology. Revo's lenses are designed to block out harmful UV rays while also providing excellent clarity and contrast. This makes them ideal for outdoor activities like fishing and boating.

I inherited a pair of vintage Revo sunglasses from my grandfather, who was an avid fisherman. The lenses are still in excellent condition and provide amazing clarity, even after all these years.

Bausch & Lomb: The Original Ray-Ban

Before Ray-Ban became the iconic brand that it is today, it was actually owned by Bausch & Lomb. The company produced sunglasses for pilots during World War II, including the famous Aviator style. In the 1990s, Bausch & Lomb sold the Ray-Ban brand to Luxottica, but vintage Bausch & Lomb sunglasses are still highly sought after by collectors.

I found a pair of vintage Bausch & Lomb sunglasses at an antique store and was blown away by their quality. The lenses are crystal clear and the frames are incredibly sturdy. They have become one of my favorite pairs of sunglasses to wear.

Question and Answer:

Q: Where can I find old sunglasses brands?

A: There are many places to find vintage sunglasses, including thrift stores, antique stores, flea markets, and online marketplaces like eBay and Etsy.

Q: How can I tell if a pair of sunglasses is vintage?

A: Look for clues like the brand name, the style, and the materials used. Vintage sunglasses may also have unique features like metal frames or unusual lens shapes.

Q: Are old sunglasses brands expensive?

A: It depends on the brand and the condition of the sunglasses. Some vintage sunglasses can be quite expensive, while others are more affordable.

Q: How do I care for my vintage sunglasses?

A: Store them in a case when not in use, avoid leaving them in direct sunlight or extreme temperatures, and clean them with a gentle lens cleaner and microfiber cloth.

Conclusion of Old Sunglasses Brands

Old sunglasses brands offer a unique and stylish alternative to the mass-produced shades that dominate the market today. Whether you are drawn to the fashion, the history, or the functionality of vintage sunglasses, there is something out there for everyone. So why not add a little bit of retro flair to your wardrobe with a pair of old sunglasses? You never know what kind of stories they may hold.
