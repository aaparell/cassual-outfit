The Dress Of Assam A Guide To Traditional Clothing

Written by Christine Jun 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Assam is a northeastern state in India known for its rich culture and heritage. One of the most significant aspects of Assamese culture is their traditional clothing. If you're wondering what is the dress of Assam, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you a comprehensive guide to Assamese traditional clothing.

Assam is a land of diverse cultures and ethnicities. The traditional dress of Assam differs from one community to another. However, the most popular and widely worn traditional attire in Assam is the Mekhela Chador for women and the Dhoti-Kurta or Gamosa for men. These dresses are not just a piece of clothing but hold immense cultural and emotional significance for the people of Assam.

The Mekhela Chador is a two-piece garment that comprises of a long skirt and a shawl-like upper garment. The skirt, known as the Mekhela, is worn around the waist and pleated at the front. The Chador, on the other hand, is draped over the shoulder and tucked on one side of the waist. The Mekhela Chador is usually made of silk or cotton and is adorned with intricate designs and patterns.

The Dhoti-Kurta is the traditional attire for men in Assam. The Dhoti is a rectangular piece of cloth worn around the waist and between the legs, while the Kurta is a long shirt that covers the upper body. The Gamosa, on the other hand, is a rectangular piece of cloth that is used as a towel, headgear, or scarf by both men and women. It is a symbol of Assamese pride and culture and is often gifted to guests as a token of respect and hospitality.

My Personal Experience with Assamese Traditional Clothing

As an Assamese myself, I have grown up wearing the Mekhela Chador on special occasions and festivals. The intricate designs and patterns on the fabric always fascinated me, and I would spend hours admiring my mother's and grandmother's collection of Mekhela Chadors. I have fond memories of watching my mother and grandmother draping the Chador perfectly, and it always amazed me how effortlessly they could do it.

Significance of Assamese Traditional Clothing

Assamese traditional clothing represents the rich cultural heritage of the state. It is a symbol of identity, pride, and tradition for the people of Assam. The intricate designs and patterns on the fabric reflect the creativity and artistic abilities of the weavers. The use of natural fibers and dyes in the making of these dresses also highlights the importance of sustainability and eco-friendliness.

The Making of Assamese Traditional Clothing

The making of Assamese traditional clothing is a time-consuming and labor-intensive process. The silk or cotton yarn is first dyed using natural dyes, and then the weaver prepares the loom for weaving. The weaving process is done manually, and it takes several days to complete a single piece of clothing. The embroidery and design work are also done by hand, and the weavers use traditional techniques and patterns to create intricate designs.

How to Wear Assamese Traditional Clothing

Wearing the Mekhela Chador or the Dhoti-Kurta is not an easy task, especially for those who are not familiar with it. However, with some practice and guidance, anyone can learn the art of draping these dresses. There are several online tutorials available that teach you how to wear the Mekhela Chador or the Dhoti-Kurta correctly. You can also seek help from your Assamese friends or family members.

Conclusion of What is the Dress of Assam

The traditional clothing of Assam is an integral part of its rich cultural heritage. The Mekhela Chador and Dhoti-Kurta are not just pieces of clothing but symbols of identity and tradition for the people of Assam. The intricate designs and patterns on the fabric reflect the creativity and artistic abilities of the weavers. The making of these dresses is a time-consuming and labor-intensive process, and it highlights the importance of sustainability and eco-friendliness. So, if you ever get a chance to visit Assam, don't forget to try out the traditional clothing and experience the rich culture and heritage of this beautiful state.
