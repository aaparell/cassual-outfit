Where To Shop For Vintage Style Clothes

Written by Bardi Sep 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old clothes you see in every store? Do you long for the unique charm of vintage style clothing? Shopping for vintage clothes can be a challenge, but with the right guidance, you can find the perfect pieces to suit your style.

One of the biggest pain points of shopping for vintage style clothes is knowing where to start. It can be overwhelming to navigate the world of secondhand clothing, and finding pieces that fit and flatter can be a challenge. But fear not, there are plenty of options available to you.

The first place to look for vintage style clothes is at your local thrift store. Thrift stores often have a wide selection of vintage and retro styles at affordable prices. You can find everything from 1920s flapper dresses to 1970s bell-bottoms. Plus, shopping at thrift stores is eco-friendly and supports local charities.

Online vintage stores are another great option for finding unique and authentic vintage pieces. Websites like Etsy, ASOS Marketplace, and Beyond Retro offer a wide range of vintage clothing from all eras. The advantage of shopping online is that you can search for specific styles and sizes, and often find pieces that are in near-perfect condition.

Vintage fairs and markets are also a great place to find vintage style clothes. These events often feature multiple vendors selling a variety of vintage and retro clothing, jewelry, and accessories. Not only can you find unique pieces, but you can also meet like-minded vintage enthusiasts and get tips on styling your finds.

Vintage Shops

One of my personal favorite places to shop for vintage style clothes is at vintage shops. These stores specialize in curating a selection of vintage and retro clothing, and often have pieces that are hard to find elsewhere. The staff are usually very knowledgeable about vintage fashion and can help you find pieces that fit your style and body type.

One of the best vintage shops I've ever been to is Rokit in London. They have a huge selection of vintage clothing from all eras, and the store is organized by decade, making it easy to find what you're looking for. They also have a great selection of vintage accessories and shoes.

Flea Markets

Another great place to find vintage style clothes is at flea markets. Flea markets are like treasure troves of vintage fashion, and you never know what you might find. One of my favorite flea markets is the Rose Bowl Flea Market in Pasadena, California. It's one of the largest flea markets in the world and features over 2,500 vendors selling everything from vintage clothing to antique furniture.

Yard Sales

Don't underestimate the power of yard sales when it comes to finding vintage style clothes. Many people sell their unwanted vintage clothing at yard sales, and you can often find great deals. Just keep an eye out for signs advertising yard sales in your neighborhood, and be prepared to do some digging.

Clothing Swaps

If you're on a tight budget, clothing swaps are a great way to update your wardrobe without spending any money. Clothing swaps involve trading clothes with friends or strangers, and can be a fun and social way to get new-to-you clothes. Many cities have clothing swap events, or you can organize one with your friends.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if a piece of clothing is vintage or just old?

A: Vintage clothing refers to clothing that is at least 20 years old and reflects the style of a certain era. Look for details like the fabric, cut, and style to determine if a piece is vintage.


Q: How do I care for vintage clothing?

A: Vintage clothing is often fragile and requires special care. Always check the care label before washing or dry cleaning, and store vintage clothes in a cool, dry place away from sunlight and moisture.


Q: How do I style vintage clothing?

A: Vintage clothing can be styled in a variety of ways, from dressy to casual. Mix vintage pieces with modern clothing for a unique look, and don't be afraid to experiment with accessories like hats and scarves.


Q: Can I alter vintage clothing to fit me better?

A: Yes, you can alter vintage clothing to fit you better, but be careful not to damage the fabric or alter the original design too much. It's best to take vintage clothing to a professional tailor who specializes in vintage clothing alterations.

Conclusion of Where to Shop for Vintage Style Clothes

Shopping for vintage style clothes can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you know where to look. Whether you prefer thrifting, online shopping, or visiting vintage shops and markets, there are plenty of options available to suit your style and budget. Just remember to take care of your vintage clothing, and enjoy the unique charm of wearing pieces from a bygone era.
