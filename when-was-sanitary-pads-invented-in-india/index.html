When Were Sanitary Pads Invented In India

Imagine not having access to a basic necessity like sanitary pads during menstruation. Unfortunately, this was the reality for many women in India for a long time. The lack of access to sanitary pads caused many health and social issues, and women suffered in silence. But when were sanitary pads invented in India, and how did they become more widely available?

For many years, women in India had to rely on old rags, leaves, or even sand and ash during menstruation. This lack of access to proper menstrual products caused many health problems, including infections, rashes, and even cervical cancer. Additionally, women faced social stigma and were often excluded from daily activities during their periods.

The first commercially available sanitary pad was invented in the United States in 1896, but it took several decades for the product to become widely available in India. In the 1970s, Indian social activist Arunachalam Muruganantham became aware of the lack of access to sanitary pads for women in rural areas. He was determined to find a solution and began experimenting with local materials to create a low-cost, reusable pad.

Muruganantham's invention was a game-changer for women in India. His low-cost pads were made from locally sourced materials and were easy to use and wash. He trained women in rural areas to make the pads themselves, providing employment opportunities and access to a basic necessity.

How to Choose the Right Sanitary Pad in India

With so many options available in the market, it can be challenging to choose the right sanitary pad. Here are a few factors to consider:

  • Size: Choose a pad that fits your body comfortably.
  • Material: Choose a pad made from breathable materials to prevent rashes and infections.
  • Brand: Look for a reputable brand that uses high-quality materials.
