Understanding Men s Dress Shirt Sizes

Written by William Nov 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever bought a dress shirt only to find out that it just doesn't fit right? Maybe it's too big in the shoulders or too tight in the chest. Understanding men's dress shirt sizes can be confusing, but it's important to know what you're looking for to ensure a proper fit.

When it comes to dress shirts, many men struggle with finding the right size. It can be frustrating to try on shirt after shirt, only to find that none of them fit quite right. This is a common pain point for many men when it comes to shopping for dress shirts.

So, what do men's dress shirt sizes mean? Dress shirt sizes typically include a neck measurement and a sleeve length. The neck measurement is the circumference of your neck, while the sleeve length is the measurement from the center back of your neck to the end of your sleeve. These measurements are used to determine the proper fit for your dress shirt.

To summarize, men's dress shirt sizes are determined by neck measurement and sleeve length. Understanding these measurements is essential for finding a dress shirt that fits properly.

Neck Measurement

When it comes to neck measurement, it's important to measure around the base of your neck, where the collar would sit. Use a cloth tape measure and make sure it's snug but not too tight. Round up to the nearest half inch if your measurement falls between two sizes.

For example, if your neck measures 16.25 inches, round up to 16.5 inches. This will ensure a comfortable fit without being too tight.

Sleeve Length

Sleeve length is measured from the center back of your neck to the end of your sleeve. It's important to note that sleeve length can vary depending on the style of shirt you're buying. For example, a dress shirt with French cuffs may require a longer sleeve length than a shirt with regular cuffs.

It's best to have someone else measure your sleeve length to ensure accuracy. Have them measure from the center back of your neck to the end of your shoulder, and then down to your wrist bone.

Additional Considerations

While neck measurement and sleeve length are the two main factors in determining dress shirt size, there are a few other factors to consider when shopping for dress shirts. These include:

  • Chest measurement
  • Waist measurement
  • Shoulder width
  • Shirt length

It's important to keep these measurements in mind when shopping for dress shirts to ensure a comfortable and proper fit.

Tips for Finding the Right Fit

Now that you understand how men's dress shirt sizes work, here are a few tips for finding the right fit:

  • Make sure the shirt fits comfortably around your neck, with enough room to insert your index and middle fingers.
  • Check that the shoulder seams sit at the edge of your shoulders, without bunching or hanging off.
  • Ensure that the shirt fits properly around your chest and waist, without being too tight or too loose.
  • Check that the sleeves are the correct length and fit properly around your arms.
  • Pay attention to the length of the shirt, making sure it's long enough to tuck in without coming untucked throughout the day.

Common Questions

Q: What if my neck and sleeve measurements fall into different sizes?

A: It's best to go with the larger size and have the shirt tailored to fit your body.

Q: How do I know if a shirt is too tight?

A: If the shirt feels restrictive around your neck, chest, or arms, it's likely too tight.

Q: Can I use a regular tape measure instead of a cloth tape measure?

A: It's best to use a cloth tape measure to ensure accuracy.

Q: How do I measure my shoulder width?

A: Have someone measure across the top of your shoulders, from the edge of one shoulder to the edge of the other.

Conclusion

Understanding men's dress shirt sizes is essential for finding a shirt that fits properly and looks great. By measuring your neck and sleeve length, as well as considering other factors like chest measurement and shirt length, you can ensure a comfortable and flattering fit. Remember to always try on dress shirts before purchasing and have them tailored if necessary for the perfect fit.
