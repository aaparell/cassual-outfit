Top 10 Grossing Boxing Matches: A Look Back at Some of the Biggest Fights in History

Boxing has always been a sport that draws in a large audience. From the thrill of the fight to the anticipation of who will come out on top, there's something about boxing that keeps people coming back for more. One of the most exciting aspects of boxing is the big fights that bring in millions of dollars in revenue. In this post, we'll take a look at the top 10 grossing boxing matches in history and explore what made them so successful.

What makes a boxing match successful? Is it the fighters involved, the hype surrounding the fight, or the location? The answer is all of the above. A successful fight needs to have two well-known fighters that have a large following, a lot of hype leading up to the fight, and a location that can accommodate a large number of people. When all of these factors come together, you have a recipe for a successful fight.

Now, let's take a closer look at the top 10 grossing boxing matches in history. These fights have set records for revenue and have gone down in history as some of the most memorable fights of all time.

1. Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Manny Pacquiao

In 2015, Floyd Mayweather Jr. and Manny Pacquiao faced off in what was dubbed "The Fight of the Century." This fight generated a record-breaking $600 million in revenue and was watched by millions of people around the world. The hype leading up to the fight was intense, with both fighters trash-talking each other in the media. Mayweather ultimately won the fight by unanimous decision.

2. Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Conor McGregor

In 2017, Floyd Mayweather Jr. came out of retirement to fight UFC superstar Conor McGregor. This fight generated $550 million in revenue and was watched by millions of people around the world. The fight was controversial, with many people feeling that McGregor was out of his depth in a boxing match. Mayweather won the fight by TKO in the 10th round.

3. Oscar De La Hoya vs. Floyd Mayweather Jr.

In 2007, Oscar De La Hoya and Floyd Mayweather Jr. faced off in a fight that generated $136 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it pitted two of the most popular fighters of the time against each other. Mayweather won the fight by split decision.

4. Mike Tyson vs. Lennox Lewis

In 2002, Mike Tyson and Lennox Lewis faced off in a fight that generated $112 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it was a battle between two heavyweight champions. Lewis won the fight by knockout in the eighth round.

5. Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Canelo Alvarez

In 2013, Floyd Mayweather Jr. fought Canelo Alvarez in a fight that generated $150 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it pitted the undefeated Mayweather against the up-and-coming Alvarez. Mayweather won the fight by majority decision.

6. Evander Holyfield vs. Mike Tyson II

In 1997, Evander Holyfield and Mike Tyson faced off in a fight that generated $100 million in revenue. This fight was particularly memorable because it was the rematch of their previous fight, which had ended in controversy. Holyfield won the fight by disqualification in the third round after Tyson bit off a piece of his ear.

7. Manny Pacquiao vs. Juan Manuel Marquez IV

In 2012, Manny Pacquiao and Juan Manuel Marquez faced off in a fight that generated $70 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it was the fourth fight in a series that had been going on for years. Marquez won the fight by knockout in the sixth round.

8. Evander Holyfield vs. Mike Tyson I

In 1996, Evander Holyfield and Mike Tyson faced off in a fight that generated $100 million in revenue. This fight was particularly memorable because Holyfield was the underdog going into the fight. Holyfield won the fight by TKO in the 11th round.

9. Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Oscar De La Hoya

In 2007, Floyd Mayweather Jr. and Oscar De La Hoya faced off in a fight that generated $136 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it pitted two of the most popular fighters of the time against each other. Mayweather won the fight by split decision.

10. Mike Tyson vs. Peter McNeeley

In 1995, Mike Tyson fought Peter McNeeley in a fight that generated $96 million in revenue. This fight was particularly exciting because it was Tyson's first fight after being released from prison.
