What are the Oldest Coffee Brands?

When it comes to the oldest coffee brands, there are several that come to mind. These brands have been around for decades, and some even for over a century. The oldest coffee brands include:

1. Lavazza

Founded in 1895 by Luigi Lavazza, Lavazza is an Italian coffee brand that has been around for over 125 years. It started as a small grocery store in Turin, Italy, and has now become a global leader in the coffee industry. Lavazza is known for its high-quality coffee blends, which are made from a combination of beans sourced from around the world.

2. Dunkin’ Donuts

Dunkin’ Donuts was founded in 1950 and has been around for over 70 years. The brand started as a coffee and donut shop in Quincy, Massachusetts, and has since grown to become one of the largest coffee chains in the world. Dunkin’ Donuts is known for its signature coffee blends, which are made using 100% Arabica beans.

3. Illy

Illy is an Italian coffee brand that was founded in 1933 by Francesco Illy. It has been around for almost 90 years and is known for its high-quality coffee blends. Illy sources its beans from countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ethiopia, and uses a unique roasting process to create its signature flavor.

4. Folgers

Folgers is an American coffee brand that was founded in 1850 by James Folger. It has been around for over 170 years and is known for its classic roast coffee blend. Folgers sources its beans from countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico, and uses a medium roast to create its signature flavor.
