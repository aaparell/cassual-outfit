What Do Indian Brides Wear on Their Head?

Choosing the perfect bridal attire is one of the most important decisions that an Indian bride has to make. The bridal outfit is incomplete without the right jewelry, makeup, and hairstyle - especially the headgear. The headgear is an essential part of an Indian bride's attire, and it is important to know what options are available to make the right choice.

Indian brides wear a variety of headgears, depending on their culture, religion, and personal preference. Some of the most popular headgears include the maang tikka, matha patti, jhoomar, passa, and the bridal dupatta.

The maang tikka is a traditional headpiece that is worn in the center of the forehead. It is usually made of gold, silver, or diamond and is one of the most popular headpieces worn by Indian brides. The matha patti is another popular headpiece that is worn across the forehead, and it is usually larger and more elaborate than the maang tikka. The jhoomar is a headpiece that is worn on the side of the head, and it is usually smaller than the matha patti. The passa is another headpiece that is worn on the side of the head, and it is usually worn with a dupatta or veil. The bridal dupatta is a long scarf that is draped over the head and is an essential part of the bridal attire.
