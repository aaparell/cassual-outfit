How Much Does A Product Designer Make A Year

Written by Daniel May 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a product designer but wondering how much you can make? It's important to know the salary range of your chosen profession to help you plan your finances and set your career goals.

According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a product designer in the United States is around $95,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and industry.

So, let's dive deeper into how much does a product designer make a year and what factors can affect their salary.

Factors That Affect a Product Designer's Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact a product designer's salary. Here are some of the most significant factors:

Experience

Product designers with more experience generally earn higher salaries. For example, a junior product designer with less than a year of experience may earn around $60,000 per year, while a senior product designer with over five years of experience can earn up to $130,000 per year.

Location

The location of the company can also impact a product designer's salary. For instance, product designers working in San Francisco, California, can earn approximately 34% more than the national average, while those working in Austin, Texas, earn about 2% less than the national average.

Industry

The industry in which a product designer works can also impact their salary. For example, product designers in the technology industry typically earn higher salaries than those in the retail industry.

Education and Skillset

Product designers with advanced degrees or specialized skills can earn higher salaries. For instance, a product designer with a master's degree in product design may earn more than one with only a bachelor's degree. Likewise, a product designer with expertise in UX/UI design can earn higher salaries than those without this skillset.

Personal Experience

As a product designer, I have worked in the industry for over five years. My starting salary was around $70,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and took on more responsibilities, my salary grew to over six figures. I also found that working in a startup versus a large corporation made a significant difference in my salary.

Salary Negotiation Tips

When negotiating your salary as a product designer, it's essential to know your worth and the industry's standards. Here are some tips to help you negotiate your salary:

Research Salaries

Before negotiating your salary, research the industry's standards and average salaries for your location and experience level. This information can help you understand what salary range to expect and what to negotiate for.

Highlight Your Skills

When negotiating your salary, highlight your skills and the value you bring to the company. Show how your skills and experience can benefit the company and why you deserve a higher salary.

Be Willing to Negotiate

Be willing to negotiate your salary and benefits. Consider other benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and vacation time, which can add value to your compensation package.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for a product designer?

A: The average starting salary for a product designer is around $60,000 per year.

Q: Can a product designer earn six figures?

A: Yes, a product designer with more experience and expertise can earn over six figures per year.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for product designers?

A: The technology industry typically pays the highest salaries for product designers.

Q: How can I negotiate my salary as a product designer?

A: Research industry standards, highlight your skills, and be willing to negotiate your compensation package.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Product Designer Make a Year

As a product designer, your salary can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, industry, education, and skillset. It's essential to research the industry standards and negotiate your salary to ensure you're getting paid what you're worth. With dedication and hard work, you can increase your salary and achieve your career goals as a product designer.
