Brands That Take Old Clothes: H&M

H&M is a popular brand that takes old clothes. They have a program called "Garment Collecting" that allows customers to drop off their old clothes in any H&M store. The clothes are then sorted and recycled into new items. H&M accepts any brand and any condition of clothes, so you don't have to worry about whether your clothes are suitable for donation. This program is available in 69 markets worldwide, making it accessible to many people.

I recently dropped off a bag of old clothes at H&M, and it was a smooth process. I received a 15% discount voucher for my next purchase, which was a nice bonus. It's great to know that my old clothes will be recycled into new items.

Brands That Take Old Clothes: Patagonia

Patagonia is another brand that takes old clothes. They have a program called "Worn Wear" that allows customers to trade in their old Patagonia clothes for store credit. The clothes are then repaired and resold, reducing waste and promoting sustainability. Patagonia also accepts old clothes from other brands, which are then donated to charity or recycled into new items. The program is available in Patagonia stores and online.

I have a couple of old Patagonia jackets that I no longer wear, so I decided to trade them in for store credit. The process was easy, and I received a fair amount of store credit. Other Brands That Take Old Clothes

Aside from H&M and Patagonia, there are other brands that take old clothes. Some of these brands include The North Face, Levi's, Eileen Fisher, and Madewell. Each brand has its own program for recycling old clothes, so it's worth checking out their websites for more information.

How to Prepare Your Old Clothes for Recycling

Before you drop off your old clothes at a recycling program, there are a few things you need to do to prepare them. First, make sure the clothes are clean and dry. Remove any hangers or tags, as these are not recyclable. If the clothes are damaged or torn, you may want to consider repairing them before recycling them. This will increase the chances of the clothes being accepted for recycling.
