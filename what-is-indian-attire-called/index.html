What is Indian Attire Called?

Indian attire is commonly known as ethnic wear or traditional wear. The term ethnic wear includes all the traditional clothing worn by people in India, and it varies from region to region. The most common types of Indian attire are sarees, salwar kameez, lehenga choli, and kurta pajama. These clothes are usually made of silk, cotton, or other traditional fabrics, and are adorned with intricate embroidery, sequins, and beads.

The Significance of Indian Attire

Indian attire is not just a piece of clothing; it is a representation of Indian culture and tradition. Each type of Indian attire has a unique history and significance behind it. For example, sarees are believed to have originated in the Indus Valley Civilization and have been worn by women in India for centuries. Similarly, the kurta pajama is a popular traditional attire for men and is often worn during weddings and other special occasions.

The Different Types of Indian Attire

As mentioned earlier, there are several types of Indian attire, each with its unique style and significance. Here are some of the most popular types of Indian attire:

  • Sarees: A saree is a long piece of cloth that is draped around the body and is worn with a blouse and petticoat.
  • Salwar Kameez: A salwar kameez is a two-piece outfit that consists of a long tunic called a kameez and loose-fitting pants called salwar.
  • Lehenga Choli: A lehenga choli is a three-piece outfit that consists of a long skirt called a lehenga, a short blouse called a choli, and a dupatta (a long scarf).
  • Kurta Pajama: A kurta pajama is a two-piece outfit that consists of a long tunic called a kurta and loose-fitting pants called pajama.

How to Choose the Right Indian Attire?

Choosing the right Indian attire can be challenging, especially if you are not familiar with the different types of clothing. Here are some tips to help you choose the right Indian attire:

  • Consider the occasion: Different types of Indian attire are suitable for different occasions. For example, sarees and lehenga cholis are often worn for weddings and other formal events, while salwar kameez and kurta pajamas are more suitable for casual occasions.
  • Choose the right fabric: Indian attire is available in a variety of fabrics, including silk, cotton, and chiffon. Choose a fabric that is comfortable and suitable for the occasion.
  • Consider your body type: Different types of Indian attire are suitable for different body types. For example, if you have a pear-shaped body, you may want to choose a salwar kameez or a lehenga choli that flatters your shape.
