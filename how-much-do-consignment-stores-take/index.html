How much do consignment stores take?

Consignment stores typically take a percentage of the selling price of an item. This percentage can vary from store to store, but it is usually around 30-50%. So, if you sell an item for $100, the consignment store may take $30-$50 as their commission.

It is important to note that some stores may have additional fees, such as a listing fee or a fee for unsold items. Make sure to ask the store about any additional fees before agreeing to consign your items.

My experience with consignment stores

As someone who has sold items at consignment stores before, I can attest to the fact that the percentage taken by the store can seem high. However, it is important to remember that the store is taking a risk by selling your item. They are responsible for displaying and marketing your item, and if it doesn't sell, they are left with unsold inventory.

When selling items at consignment stores, it is important to choose a store that aligns with your values and will attract the right customers for your items. It is also important to ensure that your items are in good condition and priced appropriately. This will increase the chances of your items selling and reduce the likelihood of them being returned to you.

Tips for successful consignment selling

Here are some tips to ensure a successful consignment selling experience:

Do your research

Before choosing a consignment store, do some research to ensure that it is a good fit for your items. Look at the store's website or visit in person to get a sense of the types of items they sell and the prices they charge.

Price your items appropriately

Make sure that your items are priced fairly and competitively. Consider the condition of the item, the original price, and the demand for the item when determining the price.
