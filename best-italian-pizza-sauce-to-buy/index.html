How to Make Your Own Italian Pizza Sauce

If you're feeling adventurous, you can also make your own Italian pizza sauce at home. Here's a simple recipe that you can try:

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Drain the juice from the can of tomatoes and crush the tomatoes with your hands.
  2. In a saucepan, heat up some olive oil and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper to the saucepan.
  4. Let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes until it thickens.
  5. Use the sauce as a base for your homemade pizza.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use marinara sauce as a pizza sauce?

A: While marinara sauce is similar to pizza sauce, it's not the ideal choice for pizza. Marinara sauce is thinner and more liquidy compared to pizza sauce, which can cause your pizza to become soggy. Pizza sauce is thicker and has a richer flavor that complements pizza toppings better.

Q: Can I use canned tomatoes for homemade pizza sauce?

A: Yes, you can use canned tomatoes for homemade pizza sauce. Just make sure to drain the juice from the can and crush the tomatoes with your hands to achieve a smoother texture.

Q: Can I freeze pizza sauce?

A: Yes, you can freeze pizza sauce for up to 3 months. Just make sure to store it in an airtight container and thaw it before using.

Q: Can I use pizza sauce as a pasta sauce?

A: Yes, you can use pizza sauce as a pasta sauce. Just add some cream or cheese to the sauce to make it creamier and more suitable for pasta dishes.
